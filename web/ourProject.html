You and your credentials


The internet as we know it has a crisis with identity, and this crisis begins and ends with the relationship between you and your credentials. Credentials, simply put, are pieces of information that identify you as an individual in our society. Items such as your passport, driver’s license, and birth certificates are all considered credentials, because they provide proof that you are who you say you are.

Before the internet, credentials almost exclusively existed as paper entities. Paper credentials work well, as they are easily verifiable. When you go to the bar and hand the server your driver’s license, they’re placing their trust into the provincial government that your credential is valid and that you are who you claim you are.

Herein lies the problem, paper credentials don’t translate well online. Credentials require trust, but the internet evolved without authorities that could be trusted reliably. This issue is monumental today because digital identity is the cornerstone of every online interaction.

Blockchain technology can provide a possible solution regarding the issue of trust. Blockchains are secure, public, and decentralized. Online credentials can be reliably verified using the Blockchain, similar to the process of verifying paper credentials.

Our project seeks to apply one potential application of Blockchain technology. We plan to create a service that will provide students and Certificate Authorities with transcripts that are digitally signed and verified through the Blockchain. With this service, authorized individuals with be able to access sensitive transcript information more easily, and securely.
